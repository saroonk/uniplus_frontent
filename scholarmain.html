Program Overview

Path of Excellence Scholarship

Supporting Saudi Arabia’s Vision 2030

The Path of Excellence Scholarship is a premier national initiative by the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia to empower talented students with world-class education. By sponsoring studies at top international universities, this program strengthens the nation’s capabilities, advances economic development, and prepares future leaders in priority fields.

Why Choose the Path of Excellence?
  • Supports top-priority career sectors under Vision 2030
  • Offers a wide range of modern and emerging fields
  • Enhances Saudi competitiveness in the global workforce
  • Develops leaders ready to elevate the Kingdom’s future

Key Objectives

  • Advance scientific and professional qualifications among Saudi youth
  • Build a competitive and innovative economy aligned with global standards
  • Support strategic and emerging sectors such as tourism, hospitality, business, and human resources
  • Enhance quality of life through fields like sports management and environmental sciences
  • Cultivate ambitious citizens who initiate growth and create meaningful opportunities for the future of the Kingdom

Building the Nation — One Scholar at a Time. The Path of Excellence Scholarship invests in the minds and skills of Saudi students today to secure a prosperous and ambitious Saudi Arabia tomorrow.

General Eligibility Requirements

Applicants must meet these conditions to secure a scholarship seat in any track.

  • Be a Saudi national.
  • Maintain good conduct and disciplinary standing.
  • Obtain unconditional direct admission to an approved Bachelor’s, Master’s, or PhD program.
  • Enroll in a full-time academic or professional program (not executive-style programs).
  • Study in the main campus country of the chosen institution.
  • Ensure consistent academic attendance.
  • Provide qualification equivalency if the previous degree was earned outside KSA.
  • Submit accurate documentation — any inconsistency may result in exclusion.

Scholarship for an Accompanying Person (Partner already received scholarship)

This scholarship is designed to support the spouse or legal guardian who accompanies a primary scholarship student abroad. The program enables the companion to pursue their own higher education qualification, thereby enhancing the family’s academic and professional development while living overseas.

It contributes to human capital growth, supports family stability, and strengthens the Kingdom’s competitiveness by empowering accompanying individuals to achieve recognized academic degrees.

Eligible companions may study a full-time Bachelor’s, Master’s, PhD, or equivalent academic program at accredited universities located in the same country as the primary student’s scholarship.

Entry Requirements:

  • The companion must have obtained the previous academic level qualification or a recognized equivalent. If the certificate was issued outside the Kingdom, equivalency is required.
  • The companion must not have already been awarded a scholarship or completed a qualification at the same academic level.
  • The primary scholarship recipient:
    • Must be on a scholarship to study an academic program (Bachelor’s, Master’s, PhD, Fellowship)
    • Must be actively studying and not in postponement status
    • Must have a scholarship decision issued as a primary student, not as a companion
  • The companion must reside together with the primary scholarship recipient in the same country and location of the university.
  • Any additional requirements may be applied based on Ministry regulations.
  • The companion’s scholarship automatically ends when the primary scholarship recipient’s mission is completed or terminated.

Specific Conditions for a Spouse or Guardian Companion

  • At least 6 months must remain in the primary scholarship’s mission period at the start of the companion’s academic study.
  • The primary scholarship student must not have previously added another companion at the same academic level.
  • The companion must secure unconditional academic admission:
    • In a specialization within the approved tracks of the Custodian of the Two Holy Mosques Scholarship Program
    • At a university recognized by the Ministry of Education
